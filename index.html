About Me

I trained as a Nutritional Therapist — graduating from the Nutritional Healing Foundation College in 2008 — and a Yoga and Meditation teacher before I had children.

Then I had three children in four years. Though my training helped, I still experienced postnatal depression, complete depletion, digestive issues, weight struggles, and a body I didn't recognise. I felt down on myself, exhausted, and like I'd lost who I was beneath the role of "mum."

"True healing isn't just about food or fitness — it's about coming home to yourself."

The Deeper Work

My nutritional therapy knowledge helped enormously, and I also sought support from a more senior health coach, counselling, and acupuncture. I learned that true healing isn't just about food or fitness — it's about coming home to yourself, and sometimes we all need support to get there.

That journey deepened my practice and eventually led me to train as a Relationship Coach, because I saw how deeply women's sense of self and their relationships — especially with themselves — are impacted by motherhood.

Your Body Knows How to Heal

Whether you're preparing for conception, optimising fertility, growing a baby, or restoring yourself after birth — every cell is designed to balance, repair, and thrive.

"Everything we eat, drink, think, and feel contributes to our internal environment."

As women and mothers, we're taught to focus outwardly — on families, responsibilities, and tasks. But when we're depleted or stressed, we often turn to quick fixes rather than sustainable solutions.

With the right nourishment and environment, your body can do what it's meant to do. My role is to help you create those conditions. When there's less stress in the body and mind, more energy is freed up for healing, repair, and growth.
